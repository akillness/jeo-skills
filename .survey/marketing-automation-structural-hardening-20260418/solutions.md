## Categories
- **Work-management / launch coordination:** Asana, Airtable-style systems, tracker-heavy planning tools
- **Template / document marketplaces:** Notion-style packet libraries
- **Execution suites:** HubSpot, Braze, Customer.io, lifecycle/CRM platforms
- **Specialist route-outs:** Steamworks/game-launch surfaces, narrow execution workflows
- **Manual substitutes:** spreadsheets, trackers, dashboards, self-built planning docs

## What People Actually Use
Teams do not solve broad marketing ambiguity with one magical marketing suite. They combine:
1. a planning/tracker surface,
2. an execution platform,
3. an analytics/readout layer,
4. ad hoc docs or spreadsheets.

That means the front-door job is usually **mode selection and packet narrowing**, not producing more channel inventory.

## Frequency Ranking
1. Work-management / tracker surfaces for launch and campaign coordination
2. Marketing suites for cross-channel execution and reporting
3. Template marketplaces for packet shapes
4. Manual spreadsheet/tracker workflows for day-to-day operator reality
5. Specialist domain docs when the ask is not actually broad marketing

## Key Gaps
- Existing tools and templates are strong **after** the team knows whether the ask is launch, conversion, lifecycle, acquisition/content, or measurement.
- Manual operator behavior still leaks into spreadsheets and trackers, which suggests the selection problem is unresolved upstream.
- A general marketing skill should therefore own **operating-mode choice + packet selection + route-outs**, not just a lane catalog.

## Contradictions
- Marketed claim: platforms promise unified cross-channel marketing.
- User reality: operators still juggle dashboards, trackers, spreadsheets, and specialist tools for different parts of the workflow.
- Marketed claim: templates make planning easy.
- User reality: templates solve artifact shape, not which artifact is the next correct one.

## Key Insight
The highest-value bounded improvement is not adding another marketing wrapper or a bigger lane inventory. It is tightening `marketing-automation` into a routing-first front door that chooses one operating mode, one primary lane, and one handoff packet before any specialist execution starts.
