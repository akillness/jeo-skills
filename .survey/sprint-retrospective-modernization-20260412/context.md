# Context: sprint-retrospective modernization

## Workflow Context
Modern retrospectives happen after sprints, releases, milestones, or failed iterations—not just at the end of textbook Scrum sprints. Teams usually run them in video calls plus a shared board or doc, while game teams often frame the same ritual as a milestone postmortem that spans engineering, design, art, QA, production, and launch work.

The recurring failure mode is not lack of templates; it is weak follow-through. Teams can produce sticky notes and votes, then lose the resulting actions because execution lives elsewhere.

## Affected Users
| Role | Responsibility | Skill Level |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| Engineering manager / tech lead | turn sprint friction into specific team improvements | medium-high |
| Scrum master / agile coach | facilitate safety, structure, and follow-through | high |
| Product manager | connect delivery issues to decision quality and stakeholder impact | medium |
| QA / design / support / ops | surface cross-functional friction and repeated pain | mixed |
| Game producer / discipline lead | run milestone postmortems across multiple specialties | medium-high |

## Current Workarounds
1. Use a board template like Start/Stop/Continue, Mad/Sad/Glad, or 4Ls in Miro, MURAL, FigJam, EasyRetro, TeamRetro, or Parabol.
2. Collect async input through Slack, forms, or docs before the meeting to reduce meeting time and improve candor.
3. Cluster duplicates and vote manually during the call.
4. Copy the winning actions into Jira, Linear, Trello, Notion, or Confluence because the retro board is not the execution system.
5. Keep a rolling spreadsheet or document section for repeated themes and unresolved retro actions.

## Adjacent Problems
- Previous retro actions are not reviewed at the start of the next retro.
- Teams reuse the same format until the ceremony goes stale.
- Evidence is fragmented across tickets, incidents, chat, docs, PRs, and customer support signals.
- Retros drift into status review instead of pattern detection and change commitments.
- Cross-functional teams need milestone or release postmortems, not only sprint-language ceremony.
