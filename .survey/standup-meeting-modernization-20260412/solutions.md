# Solution Landscape: standup-meeting modernization

## Solution List
| Name | Approach | Strengths | Weaknesses | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|-------|
| Scrum Guide / Scrum.org guidance | Facilitation framework | Canonical purpose; strong anti-status-report framing | Needs facilitator discipline; easy to regress | Best source for role and boundary clarity |
| Atlassian standup guidance / Jira template | Board-based live facilitation | Connects standup to work movement and blockers | Still manual; stale boards reduce value | Useful for board-walking patterns |
| Geekbot | Async Slack / Teams bot | Easy distributed check-ins; searchable written history | Bot fatigue; shallow answers; extra reporting layer | Verified homepage emphasizes async standups |
| Standuply | Async standups + retros + surveys in chat | Strong chat integration; broader ceremony automation | Can become process noise; still another surface to maintain | Positioned beyond standups alone |
| DailyBot | Team check-ins for software/product teams | Broad check-in support; useful for distributed teams | Duplicate reporting risk; generic responses | Positioned as team check-ins, not only standups |
| StatusHero / Steady | Lightweight status / teamwork OS | Broader visibility layer than a standup-only bot | Less focused ceremony guidance; may be overkill | Reflects the shift toward broader status systems |
| Miro daily standup template | Live visual facilitation board | Helpful for hybrid/remote ceremonies and blockers | Manual upkeep; template drift | Better for live workshops than async teams |

## Categories
- **Facilitation frameworks:** Scrum Guide, Scrum.org, Atlassian
- **Async check-in bots:** Geekbot, Standuply, DailyBot
- **Broader status platforms:** StatusHero / Steady, Range-style tools
- **Live templates / workshop boards:** Jira template, Miro template

## What People Actually Use
Many teams do not buy a dedicated standup tool at all. They either walk the existing delivery board, use Slack/Teams threads for async check-ins, or combine a chat bot with a short escalation huddle only for blockers and dependencies. The practical market signal is that *workflow shape* matters more than any specific product.

## Frequency Ranking
1. Board-walking and lightweight self-facilitated live standups
2. Async Slack / Teams standup bots
3. Jira / Miro / template-based facilitation
4. Broader status/check-in platforms

## Key Gaps
- Products automate prompts well, but they do not teach **when to use board-walking vs async vs blocker-first facilitation**.
- Many solutions add another reporting surface instead of reducing duplicate status entry.
- Existing guidance often covers the ceremony but not the **routing boundary** to adjacent PM skills like task planning, estimation, retrospectives, or issue triage.

## Contradictions
- Marketed promise: standup bots make coordination effortless.
- User reality: many teams still fall back to boards, Slack threads, and follow-up huddles because blockers and dependencies are too contextual for templated answers.

## Key Insight
The strongest modernization move is not “add more standup templates.” It is to turn `standup-meeting` into a routing/facilitation skill that helps teams choose the right standup mode (board-walk, async, blocker-first, or hybrid), keep the ritual out of status-report theater, and hand deeper work off to adjacent skills instead of trying to solve everything inside the meeting.
